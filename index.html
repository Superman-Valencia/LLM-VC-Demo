Voice conversion model based on the self-supervised large model and enhanced disentanglement strategy

East China University of Science and Technology, Shanghai, China

Abstract

The performance of auto-encoder-based voice conversion (VC) models is highly dependent on the effectiveness of the disentanglement among the content, speaker, and pitch information. In this work, we adopt the self-supervised large model and enhanced disentanglement strategy to improve the generalization of the model and the effectiveness of the disentanglement. First, the 9th and 17th embeddings extracted by the WavLM model are adopted as the input of the speaker and content encoder respectively to enhance the generalization of the input features. Second, the formants-shifting-based augmentation is introduced in the content encoding and then the cosine similarity is calculated between content embeddings before and after augmentation to eliminate redundant information in content embedding. Third, in the speaker encoding, the slicing combined with the cosine similarity loss is adopted to improve the model's ability to learn the global feature, and the AM-Softmax loss is introduced to enlarge the inter-speaker distance. Experimental results on VCTK demonstrated that the proposed model achieves good performance in terms of objective and subjective evaluations under both seen and unseen scenarios.
