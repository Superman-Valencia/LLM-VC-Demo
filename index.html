Abstract

One-shot voice conversion (VC) is always challenging, especially in an unseen scenario. This paper proposes a VC model based on the pre-trained large language model and a specific disentanglement strategy. On the one hand, the embeddings extracted by specific layers of pre-trained WavLM are employed as input for the speaker and content encoders, respectively, to improve the model's generalization and simplify the architecture of the two encoders. On the other hand, a specific disentanglement strategy is proposed to eliminate redundant information in embeddings obtained by WavLM. i) The formant-shifting-based augmentation loss is adopted to reduce content-irrelevant information effectively in content encoding; ii) The AM-Softmax loss is introduced to adjust the distance between different speakers; iii) L1 loss is calculated in pitch and mel-spectrogram reconstruction to improve the converted speech's quality further. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed model achieves excellent performance in objective and subjective evaluations under both seen and unseen scenarios.
